<p><p>The acrylic fiber market comprises staple fiber, tows, and tops, each serving distinct applications. Staple fiber, short and versatile, is used in textiles like sweaters and blankets. Tows, continuous filament bundles, are processed into staple fibers or used in non-woven fabrics. Tops, semi-processed fibers aligned for spinning, are ideal for high-quality yarns in apparel and home textiles. These types cater to diverse industries, offering durability, softness, and resistance to sunlight and chemicals, making acrylic fibers a popular choice in global markets.</p></p>
