<p><p>The fire-resistant fabric market comprises two primary types: inherent fire-resistant fabrics and treated fire-resistant fabrics. Inherent fire-resistant fabrics are made from fibers that have natural fire-resistant properties, providing durable protection against flames and heat without requiring additional treatments. In contrast, treated fire-resistant fabrics are regular fibers that have undergone chemical processes to enhance their flame resistance. Both types are essential in various industries, including construction, automotive, and protective clothing, ensuring safety and compliance with fire safety standards.</p></p>
