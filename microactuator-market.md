<p><p>The microactuator market features several key types, each leveraging different principles for movement. Electrostatic actuators use electric fields to create motion, ideal for applications requiring precision. Electromagnetic actuators generate forces through magnetic fields, often used in larger applications. Piezoelectric actuators convert electrical energy into mechanical motion, suited for fine control. Fluid actuators manipulate hydraulic or pneumatic pressure for robust movements, while thermal actuators rely on temperature changes to drive movement, offering unique solutions for various technological needs.</p></p>
