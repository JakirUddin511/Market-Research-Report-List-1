<p><p>The battery energy storage market encompasses various technologies, primarily Li-Ion, Lead-Acid, and Sodium-Sulfur batteries. Li-Ion batteries dominate due to their high energy density and efficiency, making them ideal for renewable energy integration and electric vehicles. Lead-Acid batteries, while heavier and less efficient, are cost-effective and widely used for backup power and short-term storage. Sodium-Sulfur batteries offer high energy capacity and long discharge times, suitable for large-scale applications, but are less common due to higher costs and complexities in operation.</p></p>
