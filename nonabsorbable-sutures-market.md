<p><p>The nonabsorbable sutures market is categorized into two main types: natural and synthetic. Natural nonabsorbable sutures, typically made from organic materials such as silk or cotton, are known for their biocompatibility and ease of handling, but may be prone to tissue reactions. Synthetic nonabsorbable sutures, made from materials like nylon or polypropylene, offer greater tensile strength, durability, and lower risk of infection, making them suitable for a wide range of surgical applications. Both types are essential in wound closure and tissue approximation.</p></p>
