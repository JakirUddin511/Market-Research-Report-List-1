<p><p>The synthetic peptide vaccine market is primarily segmented into two synthesis methods: Solution Phase Synthesis (SPS) and Solid Phase Peptide Synthesis (SPPS). SPS involves synthesizing peptides in a liquid solution, allowing for easier purification and modification. In contrast, SPPS synthesizes peptides attached to a solid support, enabling streamlined purification and scalability. Both methods are crucial for producing tailored peptides for vaccines, with distinct advantages in efficiency, cost, and versatility, responding to the growing demand for targeted immunotherapies.</p></p>
