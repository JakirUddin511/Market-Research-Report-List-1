<p><p>The handheld dermatoscopes market is divided into two main types: contact dermatoscopes and non-contact dermatoscopes. Contact dermatoscopes require direct skin contact for imaging, providing enhanced clarity and detail, making them ideal for examining small lesions. Non-contact dermatoscopes, on the other hand, capture images without physical contact, allowing for a broader view of the skin and minimizing patient discomfort. Both types are essential tools in dermatology for accurate diagnosis and monitoring of skin conditions.</p></p>
