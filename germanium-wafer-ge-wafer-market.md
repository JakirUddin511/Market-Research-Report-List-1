<p><p>The Germanium wafer market features two primary types based on electrical conductivity: P-type and N-type. P-type Germanium wafers are doped with elements that create "holes" or positive charge carriers, enabling efficient hole conduction. Conversely, N-type wafers are doped with elements that provide excess electrons, facilitating electron conduction. Both types are essential in the semiconductor industry, particularly for applications in electronic devices, photovoltaic cells, and high-frequency applications, each catering to specific electrical performance requirements.</p></p>
