<p><p>Ceramic-based membrane filtration systems are categorized into three main types: microfiltration, hyperfiltration, and nanofiltration. Microfiltration systems remove larger particles and microorganisms, making them suitable for water treatment and food processing. Hyperfiltration, or reverse osmosis, targets smaller solutes, effectively desalinating water and purifying beverages. Nanofiltration sits between the two, removing multivalent ions and small organic molecules, finding applications in wastewater treatment and softening water. Each type serves distinct needs within industries ranging from environmental to pharmaceutical.</p></p>
