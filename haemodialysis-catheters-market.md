<p><p>Haemodialysis catheters are specialized medical devices used in patients with kidney failure to facilitate the removal of waste, excess fluid, and toxins from the blood. This growth is driven by several factors, including the rising prevalence of chronic kidney disease (CKD), increasing awareness regarding early diagnosis and treatment, and technological advancements in catheter designs, leading to improved patient outcomes and comfort. Moreover, the aging population, which is more susceptible to kidney-related disorders, further contributes to market expansion.</p><p>Recent trends indicate a shift towards the development of innovative and biocompatible materials for catheter production, as well as the incorporation of features aimed at minimizing complications such as infections and thrombosis. <p><p>Haemodialysis catheters are essential for patients with kidney failure requiring dialysis. The market features three primary types: **Cuffed Tunneled Catheters**, which are surgically placed under the skin and have a cuff to minimize infection risks; **Non-cuffed Tunneled Catheters**, similar but without the cuff, offering flexibility for longer-term use; and **Non-tunneled Catheters**, typically used for short-term access, inserted directly into a vein. Each type addresses specific patient needs based on the duration of dialysis treatment and infection management.</p></p>
