<p><p>Protein stability analysis encompasses various market types, including chromatography, spectroscopy, surface plasmon resonance (SPR), and differential scanning fluorimetry (DSF). Chromatography techniques assess the purity and stability of proteins by separating them based on various characteristics. Spectroscopy methods, like dynamic light scattering (DLS) and fluorescence spectroscopy, provide insights into protein conformation and interaction dynamics. SPR enables real-time monitoring of protein interactions, while DSF evaluates thermal stability, allowing researchers to understand protein behavior under varying conditions and enhance drug development processes.</p></p>
