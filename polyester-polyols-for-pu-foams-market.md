<p><ul><li>Vacuum Melting Method</li><li>Carrier Gas Melting Method</li><li>Azeotropic Vapor Method</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Polyester polyols for polyurethane (PU) foams are primarily categorized by their production methods. The vacuum melting method involves melting polyester under reduced pressure, minimizing oxidation and improving purity. The carrier gas melting method utilizes an inert gas to facilitate the melting process, enhancing safety and efficiency. The azeotropic vapor method employs a solvent that forms an azeotrope with water, allowing for effective removal during the polyol synthesis. Each method influences the properties and applications of the resultant polyester polyols in PU foams.</p></p>
