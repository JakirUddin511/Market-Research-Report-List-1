<p><p>The pea protein powder market primarily consists of two types: pea protein isolates and pea protein concentrates. Pea protein isolates undergo a more extensive processing method to remove most of the starch and fiber, resulting in a higher protein content of around 90%. In contrast, pea protein concentrates retain more of the natural nutrients, with protein content typically ranging from 70-80%. These differences make isolates suitable for high-protein formulations, while concentrates are often used for their additional nutrients and cost-effectiveness.</p></p>
