<p><p>The VLSI semiconductor market comprises various memory types, each catering to specific needs. MRAM (Magnetoresistive Random Access Memory) offers non-volatile storage with fast speeds, ideal for data integrity. SRAM (Static Random Access Memory) provides high-speed memory for cache applications due to its quick access time but is volatile. DRAM (Dynamic Random Access Memory) is widely used in computing for its high density and cost-effectiveness but is also volatile. Flash ROM offers non-volatile storage, widely used in portable devices for reliable data retention.</p></p>
