<p><ul><li>Absorbable Sutures</li><li>Non-absorbable Sutures</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Operation Suture involves the use of materials to close wounds or surgical incisions. The market is primarily divided into two types: absorbable sutures, which are designed to be gradually broken down and absorbed by the body, eliminating the need for removal, and non-absorbable sutures, which remain in the body until surgically removed or are permanently retained. Each type serves specific clinical purposes, with absorbable sutures often used for internal tissues and non-absorbable sutures preferred for external skin closure.</p></p>
