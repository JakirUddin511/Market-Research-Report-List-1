<p><p>The inorganic soil redeposition market consists of four primary types: sand, clay, loam, and silt. Sand is coarse, promoting drainage and aeration, making it ideal for construction and landscaping. Clay, fine and dense, is used for its plasticity and water retention in agriculture. Loam, a balanced mixture of sand, silt, and clay, supports diverse plant growth, often favored in gardening. Silt, with fine particles, retains moisture and nutrients, enhancing agricultural soils. Each type serves distinct environmental and commercial applications.</p></p>
