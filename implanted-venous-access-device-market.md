<p><p>The Implanted Venous Access Device market comprises various types, including Peripheral Inserted Central Catheters (PICC), which are inserted through the arm and accessed via central veins. Centrally Inserted Catheters are categorized into non-tunneled, placed directly into a major vein, and tunneled, which are designed to be long-term with a portion left under the skin. Implanted ports are devices implanted under the skin for repeated access. Other devices include subcutaneous devices and specialty catheters, enhancing treatment in various medical conditions.</p></p>
