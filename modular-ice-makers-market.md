<p><p>Modular ice makers are essential appliances for various commercial settings, and they are primarily categorized into two types: water-cooled and air-cooled ice makers. Water-cooled ice makers use water for cooling, making them efficient in high-temperature environments, but they require a constant water supply. In contrast, air-cooled ice makers rely on ambient air for cooling, making them easier to install and maintain but less efficient in hot conditions. Each type offers distinct advantages based on operational needs and environmental factors.</p></p>
