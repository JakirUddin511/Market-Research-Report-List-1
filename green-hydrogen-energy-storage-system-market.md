<p><p>Green hydrogen energy storage systems can be categorized into three market types: gas, liquid, and solid. Gas storage typically involves compressing hydrogen in high-pressure tanks, allowing for efficient energy retention and transport. Liquid storage involves converting hydrogen into a cryogenic liquid state, facilitating higher density storage but requiring special cryogenic systems. Solid storage utilizes materials like metal hydrides to absorb and release hydrogen, providing a stable and compact option. Each method offers distinct advantages depending on application and energy needs.</p></p>
